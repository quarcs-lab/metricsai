## Key Principles Demonstrated in CH11 Enhancements

### 1. Use Actual Numbers
- Every interpretation references specific values from the output
- Example: "Size coefficient = $68.37 (p < 0.001)" not "Size is significant"

### 2. Explain Statistical Concepts
- p-values explained as probabilities
- Confidence intervals explained with repeated sampling interpretation
- F-statistics explained as ratios of variation

### 3. Connect to Economics
- What does a coefficient mean in dollars and cents?
- Why might bedrooms not matter after controlling for size?
- What does multicollinearity imply about the housing market?

### 4. Guide Decision-Making
- Which model should we use?
- When to use robust standard errors?
- How to interpret model comparison results?

### 5. Highlight Surprises
- Bedrooms not significant (counterintuitive)
- Five additional variables add almost nothing
- Simpler model is better despite lower R²
