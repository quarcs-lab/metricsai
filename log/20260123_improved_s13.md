## Chapter 13 Coverage

**Case Study 13.1: School Performance Index**
- California Academic Performance Index (API) data for 807 high schools
- Bivariate analysis: 80 API points per year of parent education
- Multiple regression: 74 points after controlling for meals, English learners, year-round, teacher credentials
- High correlations among socioeconomic variables
- Challenge of isolating effects of educational inputs from socioeconomic factors

**Case Study 13.2: Cobb-Douglas Production Function**
- U.S. manufacturing data 1899-1922
- Natural logarithm transformation to linearize model
- Estimated elasticities: capital 0.233, labor 0.807
- Testing constant returns to scale (elasticities sum to 1.040)
- Testing Cobb-Douglas original values (0.25 and 0.75)
- Prediction with retransformation bias correction
- HAC-robust standard errors for time series

**Case Study 13.3: Phillips Curve**
- U.S. annual data 1949-2014 on unemployment and inflation
- Original Phillips curve: negative relationship pre-1970
- Breakdown post-1970: positive (insignificant) relationship
- Augmented Phillips curve: adding expected inflation resolves breakdown
- Classic example of omitted variables bias
- Policy implications: limited long-run trade-off

**Case Study 13.4: Automobile Efficiency**
- Annual data on cars and light trucks 1980-2006 (n=27,871)
- Log-log model of fuel efficiency
- Efficiency gains negated by heavier, more powerful vehicles
- Cluster-robust standard errors by manufacturer

**Case Study 13.5: Rand Health Insurance Experiment**
- 1970s randomized control trial
- 20,203 individual-year observations
- Better insurance increases health spending
- Causal estimate from random assignment
- Cluster-robust standard errors by family

**Case Study 13.6: Access to Health Care and Health Status**
- 1994 South Africa policy change
- Difference-in-differences method
- Increased clinic access improves child health (weight-for-age z-score)
- Treatment: children in communities with clinics
- Cluster-robust standard errors by community

**Case Study 13.7: Gains to Political Incumbency**
- U.S. Senate elections 1914-2010 (n=1,390)
- Regression discontinuity design
- 5-7% vote share advantage from winning previous election
- Compares outcomes just above vs. just below winning threshold

**Case Study 13.8: Institutions and Country GDP**
- 64 countries settled by Europeans
- Instrumental variables estimation
- Instrument: log settler mortality
- Better institutions causally increase GDP

**Case Study 13.9: From Raw Data to Final Data**
- Data carpentry/wrangling workflow
- Reading data from multiple formats (Excel, CSV, PDF, web)
- Merging data from multiple sources
- Data cleaning: recoding, error detection
- Validation at multiple stages
