## Key Econometric Concepts Covered

### Extension from Bivariate to Multiple Regression
- T(n-k) distribution replaces T(n-2) distribution
- k = total number of regressors including intercept
- Same inference logic but different degrees of freedom
- Individual tests now "control for" other regressors

### F Tests and Joint Hypotheses
- Test multiple restrictions simultaneously
- Cannot use t-tests for joint hypotheses
- F distribution with two degrees of freedom
- Overall significance test: all slopes = 0
- Subset tests: groups of regressors jointly significant

### Multicollinearity and Precision
- Perfect multicollinearity: cannot estimate coefficients
- Imperfect multicollinearity: inflates standard errors
- Variance depends on residuals xÃÉ‚±º·µ¢ from auxiliary regression
- High correlation among regressors reduces precision

- Unbiased: E[b‚±º] = Œ≤‚±º (assumptions 1-2)
- Consistent: b‚±º ‚Üí Œ≤‚±º as n ‚Üí ‚àû
- BLUE: Best linear unbiased estimator (assumptions 1-4)
