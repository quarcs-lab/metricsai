## Chapter 12 Coverage

**Section 12.1: Inference with Robust Standard Errors**
- Three types: heteroskedastic-robust, cluster-robust, HAC-robust
- Heteroskedastic-robust for cross-section with varying error variance
- Cluster-robust for grouped data (villages, panel data)
- HAC-robust for time series with autocorrelation
- Implementation using Stata vce() option or R sandwich package
- Degrees of freedom: G-1 for cluster-robust, n-k for others

**Section 12.2: Prediction**
- Distinction between conditional mean and individual forecast
- Variance formulas: Var(≈∑_f) = Var(≈∑_cm) + Var(u*)
- Conditional mean prediction intervals (narrow)
- Individual forecast intervals (wide, at least ¬±1.96√ós_e)
- Why forecasts are imprecise even with precise estimates
- Policy implications: focus on average outcomes

**Section 12.3: Nonrepresentative Samples**
- Sampling on y causes bias
- Control variables for observed characteristics
- Sample weights for weighted least squares

**Section 12.4: Best Estimation**
- Unbiased and consistent estimators
- Best estimator: smallest variance
- FGLS when assumptions 3-4 fail
- Practical use of OLS with robust SE

**Section 12.5: Best Confidence Intervals**
- Shortest width at given confidence level
- Requires most efficient estimator

**Section 12.6: Best Tests: Type I and II Errors**
- Type I error (false positive), Type II error (false negative)

- Most powerful test uses most efficient estimator
- Trade-off between error types

**Section 12.7: Data Science and Big Data**
